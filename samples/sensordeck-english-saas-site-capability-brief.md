## Proposed six-page information architecture

The following structure is a working architecture. Product claims, customer segments, integrations, security statements, metrics, logos, and testimonials remain placeholders until SensorDeck supplies verifiable source material.

### 1. Home

Purpose: explain the product in one screen, route visitors to the right detail, and create one clear conversion path.

Sections:

1. Hero with one audience, one operational outcome, and one primary call to action
2. Verified proof strip using supplied facts only
3. Three core product capabilities
4. Primary buyer problem and resulting workflow improvement
5. Solutions preview by use case or audience
6. Three-step workflow preview
7. Product screenshot or interface preview supplied by SensorDeck
8. Final call to action

### 2. Product

Purpose: connect product capabilities to practical buyer value.

Sections:

1. Product promise and category definition
2. Capability blocks with interface evidence
3. Data input, processing, and output overview
4. Integration or deployment facts supplied by SensorDeck
5. Security, privacy, and data handling statements approved by the responsible owner
6. Product FAQ
7. Walkthrough call to action

### 3. Solutions

Purpose: help each qualified visitor recognize a relevant use case.

Sections:

1. Primary audience or industry selector
2. One problem, one workflow, and one outcome for each approved use case
3. Relevant product capabilities
4. Implementation conditions and exclusions
5. Verified evidence or product screenshot
6. Contact call to action

No industry, customer, savings, uptime, accuracy, or ROI claim will be invented. SensorDeck must approve every use case and proof statement.

### 4. Workflow

Purpose: reduce uncertainty about adoption and daily use.

Sections:

1. Workflow overview
2. Step 1: connect or provide the approved input
3. Step 2: configure the relevant rule, view, or alert
4. Step 3: review the resulting signal or output
5. Step 4: hand off the next action
6. Roles and responsibilities
7. Expected setup inputs
8. Walkthrough call to action

The final workflow will follow SensorDeck's real product behavior. No dashboard interaction or automation will be implied without product evidence.

### 5. About

Purpose: provide credible context without overstating company maturity.

Sections:

1. Company mission in plain English
2. Problem the team chose to solve
3. Product principles
4. Team facts approved for publication
5. Company location, legal entity, and contact facts supplied by SensorDeck
6. Contact call to action

### 6. Contact

Purpose: collect enough context for one useful follow-up.

Sections:

1. Short expectation-setting introduction
2. Contact form
3. Response-time statement approved by SensorDeck
4. Direct business email supplied by SensorDeck
5. Privacy notice link supplied or approved by SensorDeck
6. Success, error, and retry states

## Above-the-fold English copy direction

The public project page does not disclose enough verified product detail to write a factual final claim. The first design pass can use the following message frame and replace bracketed fields after the product interview.

Eyebrow:

`SensorDeck for [primary buyer or operating team]`

Headline direction A:

`See the signal. Decide sooner. Act with confidence.`

Headline direction B:

`Turn [verified input] into decisions your team can act on.`

Supporting copy:

`SensorDeck helps [verified audience] move from [current friction] to [approved operational outcome] with a clear workflow for [core product action].`

Primary call to action:

`Book a product walkthrough`

Secondary call to action:

`See how SensorDeck works`

Optional proof line:

`Built for [verified market] · Works with [verified integration] · [approved security or deployment fact]`

The proof line stays hidden until every item is documented. The page will not use placeholder customer logos, fabricated quotes, unsupported superlatives, or invented performance figures.

## Visual and interaction direction

The proposed direction is a clear technical B2B system with restrained color, strong typography, readable diagrams, product interface evidence, and deliberate spacing. The visual language should help a technical buyer understand the product before adding decorative motion.

One coherent component system would cover navigation, hero, proof strip, capability cards, use-case blocks, workflow steps, FAQ, form, final call to action, and footer. Motion remains limited to lightweight interaction feedback and respects reduced-motion settings.

## Responsive boundary

Included:

1. Six public marketing routes and one shared navigation and footer system
2. Responsive layouts checked at representative 1440, 768, and 390 pixel widths
3. No horizontal overflow at the agreed viewport set
4. Keyboard-visible focus states for links, buttons, navigation, and form controls
5. Semantic headings, descriptive links, labelled inputs, and alt text for supplied images
6. Current stable desktop and mobile versions of Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge
7. Responsive image sizing and deferred loading where appropriate

Excluded unless separately scoped:

1. Logged-in product screens or a functioning SensorDeck dashboard
2. Legacy Internet Explorer support
3. Full WCAG certification or legal accessibility review
4. Custom 3D, video production, illustration production, or complex animation
5. A CMS, multilingual content system, blog, customer portal, or account authentication
6. Product analytics configuration, consent management, or legal compliance approval

## Contact form boundary

The base form contains Name, Work Email, Company, Role, and What are you evaluating. One approved consent or privacy line can be displayed if SensorDeck supplies the final text and privacy URL.

Included form behavior:

1. Required-field and email-format validation
2. Loading, success, validation error, and delivery error states
3. A simple spam-reduction measure such as a honeypot
4. One buyer-authorized end-to-end test using a redacted test submission
5. Delivery to one approved business inbox
6. No secret key committed to the public repository or exposed in the browser bundle

Form delivery can use SensorDeck's approved Vercel function, Resend account, Formspree account, or another agreed provider. SensorDeck retains account ownership and supplies access through its own approved method. Provider fees, email deliverability, CRM integration, automated sequences, and legal consent wording remain outside the base scope.

## Acceptance checklist

1. Home, Product, Solutions, Workflow, About, and Contact routes are deployed and reachable from the main navigation.
2. The agreed production build completes without an unresolved build error.
3. Navigation, calls to action, internal links, and approved external links work on staging and production.
4. The agreed 1440, 768, and 390 pixel checks show no unintended horizontal overflow or obstructed primary action.
5. Each page has one unique title and meta description. Canonical and social preview fields use the approved production domain.
6. Supplied images have appropriate alt text or are marked decorative.
7. Interactive elements have visible keyboard focus and form inputs have programmatic labels.
8. Contact form validation, loading, success, and error states are demonstrated.
9. One redacted buyer-authorized form test arrives in the approved inbox, subject to the selected provider being configured and operational.
10. No approved private key or secret appears in the public client bundle or repository history created during this project.
11. SensorDeck approves the final English claims, company facts, product screenshots, privacy link, and contact details.
12. Source code is delivered to the agreed GitHub repository with setup, build, deployment, environment variable, and content-edit notes.
13. The agreed Vercel project and domain are connected using SensorDeck-owned accounts.
14. A final known-limitations note records any deferred content, provider dependency, browser exception, or future enhancement.

Lighthouse and automated checks can be recorded during delivery, but this brief does not promise a fixed performance, SEO, accessibility, traffic, lead, or revenue score.
